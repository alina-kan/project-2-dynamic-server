Exploration of Global Power Plants and Energy

By Lizzie Paterson, Andy Phan, and Alina Kanayinkal

 

The Global Power Plant Database is an open source database of many types of power plants around the world. It has lots of different information regarding the powerplants, such as the longitude and latitude of the plant, the country of origin, plant capacity, and more. It also covers around 35,000 power plants from 167 countries, focusing on thermal and renewable plants. We explored the locations of the power plants, the energy source used by the plant, and the plant capacity.

   Overview of Website:

  • Location:

    • This page features a table looking at the specified country, the plant name located in that country, the longitude and latitude of the plant, the energy source used by that plant, and the capacity of the specified plant. The information is found using the abbreviated version of the country's name.

  • Energy Source:

    • This page features a table looking at the countries using the specified energy source, the plant name using that energy source, the specified energy source used in primary electricity generation or export, and the capacity of the plant. The page also features a pie chart that specifies what energy source is shown and how many plants use them in percentages. The information is found using the abbreviated version of the energy source's name.

  • Capacity:

    • This page features a table looking at the countries in that capacity range, the plant name of those having capacities in range, the actual capacity, and the energy source used to produce electricity, which determines the capacity. The information is found by using the "low", "medium", and "high" ranges.

   Connection to Sustainability

Power plants are facilities designed to produce electricity from other energy sources, such as coal and oil. However, many of the popular and more lucrative energy sources are nonrenewable resources that contribute to multiple kinds of pollution, such as water and air pollution. There are renewable resources that can be used in order to produce as much electric energy as their nonrenewable counterparts, such as solar energy and wind energy. The use of renewable energy has grown in many power plants, as it is just as efficient as nonrenewable resources and these renewable resources are better for the environemt as they don't produce as much pollution or byproduct. Looking at the pie chart, the top three resources are Solar Energy, Hydrolic Energy, and Wind Energy, which are all renewable resources with a little byproduct and lower pollution rates. As more power plants begin to use renewable resources, the more we will be able to sustainabilly produce energy.
